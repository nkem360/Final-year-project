PET HEALTH KNOWLEDGE BASE - SAMPLE ENTRIES
==========================================
Source: General Veterinary Reference

DOGS - COMMON CONDITIONS
-------------------------

Limping / Lameness
- Causes: paw injury, thorn/splinter in paw, muscle strain, joint pain, arthritis, fracture,
  ligament tear (CCL/ACL), Lyme disease (tick bite)
- Urgency: moderate if weight-bearing, high if non-weight-bearing or severe pain
- Home care: rest, check paw for debris, ice wrapped in cloth for 10 min if swollen
- See vet if: limping persists >24h, joint is swollen, pet refuses to walk, fever present

Loss of Appetite (Anorexia)
- Causes: stress, change in food, dental pain, upset stomach, infection, kidney disease,
  liver disease, parvovirus (puppies), obstruction
- Urgency: low if <24h in otherwise healthy adult dog; high if puppy, senior, or >48h
- Home care: offer bland diet (boiled chicken + rice), ensure fresh water
- See vet if: >48h, vomiting/diarrhea present, lethargy, weight loss, black/tarry stool

Vomiting
- Causes: eating too fast, dietary indiscretion (ate garbage), food intolerance, parasites,
  parvovirus, pancreatitis, obstruction, kidney/liver disease, bloat (GDV)
- EMERGENCY signs: bloated abdomen + retching without vomiting = possible GDV → vet NOW
- Home care: fast 6–12h, then bland diet; ensure hydration
- See vet if: >3 episodes/day, blood in vomit, severe lethargy, known toxin ingestion

Diarrhea
- Causes: dietary change, stress, parasites (giardia, roundworm), bacterial infection,
  viral illness, IBD, food allergy
- Home care: bland diet, probiotics, ensure hydration
- See vet if: blood in stool, >48h, severe lethargy, puppy or senior dog, suspected parvovirus

Lethargy / Low Energy
- Causes: infection, anemia, pain, heartworm, metabolic disease, hypothyroidism, depression
- See vet if: persists >48h, combined with other symptoms

Itching / Skin Irritation
- Causes: fleas, environmental allergies, food allergies, mange, hot spots, fungal infection
- Home care: check for fleas, gentle oatmeal bath
- See vet if: raw/open skin, hair loss, crusting, no improvement after 3 days

CATS - COMMON CONDITIONS
-------------------------

Urinary Issues
- EMERGENCY: male cats straining to urinate with no output = urinary blockage → vet IMMEDIATELY
- Causes: UTI, crystals/stones, stress (FIC), obstruction
- Signs of blockage: frequent trips to litter box, vocalising in pain, no urine, lethargy
- See vet: any male cat straining to urinate urgently

Not Eating
- Causes: stress, dental pain, respiratory infection (can't smell food), kidney disease,
  hyperthyroidism, cancer
- Note: cats should not fast >48h → risk of hepatic lipidosis (fatty liver disease)
- See vet if: >24h in sick cat, >48h in any cat

Respiratory Symptoms (sneezing, runny nose, watery eyes)
- Causes: feline upper respiratory infection (herpesvirus, calicivirus), allergies, foreign body
- Home care: steam from shower, keep nares clear, encourage eating with smelly food
- See vet if: laboured breathing, open-mouth breathing (EMERGENCY), no eating

Hairballs
- Normal if occasional; concern if frequent vomiting, constipation, loss of appetite
- Home care: hairball remedy paste, regular brushing

EMERGENCY SYMPTOMS - ALL SPECIES
----------------------------------
These symptoms require IMMEDIATE veterinary attention:
- Seizures or convulsions
- Difficulty breathing / gasping / open-mouth breathing
- Collapse or unconsciousness
- Suspected poisoning (chocolate, grapes, raisins, xylitol, antifreeze, rat poison, medications)
- Severe or uncontrolled bleeding
- Trauma (hit by car, fall from height, animal attack)
- Bloated or distended abdomen (dogs especially - possible GDV)
- Male cat straining to urinate with no output
- Pale, white, blue, or yellow gums
- Inability to stand or severe sudden weakness
- Eye prolapse

COMMON TOXIC SUBSTANCES FOR PETS
----------------------------------
Dogs and cats:
- Chocolate (especially dark/baking chocolate) → theobromine toxicity
- Grapes and raisins → kidney failure
- Xylitol (artificial sweetener) → hypoglycaemia, liver failure
- Onions and garlic → red blood cell damage
- Macadamia nuts → weakness, tremors (dogs)
- Antifreeze (ethylene glycol) → kidney failure (sweet taste attracts pets)
- Rat/mouse poison → internal bleeding, neurological damage
- Human NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) → kidney and GI damage
- Acetaminophen (paracetamol/Tylenol) → liver damage (especially cats)
- Alcohol → hypoglycaemia, CNS depression

NUTRITION BASICS
----------------
Dogs:
- Adult dogs: 2 meals/day; puppies: 3–4 meals/day
- Fresh water always available
- Avoid: cooked bones (splinter risk), high-fat foods (pancreatitis risk)

Cats:
- Obligate carnivores — require animal protein
- Wet food supports urinary health
- Do not feed dog food long-term (lacks taurine)
- Avoid: raw fish in excess (thiamine deficiency), milk (lactose intolerance common)

PREVENTIVE CARE SCHEDULE
-------------------------
Dogs:
- Vaccinations: distemper, parvovirus, adenovirus (core); rabies (legal requirement)
- Parasite prevention: monthly flea/tick/heartworm prevention year-round
- Dental: annual dental check, regular tooth brushing
- Annual vet exam; senior dogs (7+) every 6 months

Cats:
- Vaccinations: FVRCP (core); rabies; FeLV if outdoor
- Parasite prevention: monthly flea prevention (indoor cats too)
- Annual vet exam; senior cats (10+) every 6 months
- Spay/neuter recommended (reduces cancer, infection risk)
